Desert Gear: As a southern Californian, I’m surrounded by numerous hiking opportunities. Whether that’s the Los Padres national Forest, Angeles National Forest, San Bernardino National Forest, or even some parts of Inyo National Forest, these environments require specific gear (things that allow for making extra room for extra water). Because you’re often not dealing with cold temperatures, there’s little need to bring the heavier warm items, as you wouldn’t want to bring more than you need to. So, this set up will include the lighter items of my available options. Click here to see my set up for desert adventures.

Alpine Gear/Glacier Lake Gear: A good part of being a Californian is learning about the majesty of the High Sierras (for me personally, I’ve mainly encountered the eastern side of the Sierra-Nevadas). For this environment, you’ll likely be climbing thousands of feet over treacherous rocky passes to get to the absolutely beautiful glacier lake sceneries. So, making sure you don’t bring more than you need to would be a nice thing to do for your poor legs. Also, most of the wilderness areas in the Sierras require that you bring items like Bear Cans, so adjusting what to bring is a must. Also, it would be a crying shame to hike all those thousands of feet to a beautiful glacier lake and not go fishing for trout, so this gear set up will, of course, include fly fishing gear.
